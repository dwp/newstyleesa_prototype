Send us supporting medical evidence

From 10 July, you will need to send us medical evidence to support your application. We accept these medical certificates:

  • Fit note (also called a statement of fitness for work or a sick note)
  • Med10 (a hospital certificate given to inpatients if your treatment lasts more than 7 days)
  • MATB1 (if you're pregnant but you cannot get Statutory Maternity Pay or Maternity Allowance)
  • CF73 (a form issued by a psychiatric hospital)
  • DS1500 (a form completed by a doctor, consultant or specialist nurse if they think you might not live longer than 6 months, because of your condition)

Other forms of evidence might include a doctor's statement, consultant's report or Local Authority notice.

You do not need to send medical evidence if you're claiming New Style ESA because of coronavirus.

How to send your evidence

If you have a fit note that's signed by your doctor, you can use the Send Your Fit Note service (opens in a new window) to send it to us online.

If you have another form of medical evidence, you can send it to us by post. You can also post your fit note.
