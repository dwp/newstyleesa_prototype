Sharing information about your health condition

We (the Department for Work and Pensions), or healthcare professionals approved by the Secretary of State, might need to ask for information about your health condition and how it affects you.

We might ask for this information from:

  • your doctor
  • hospital
  • other relevant professionals that you tell us about

They need your agreement (this is called your consent) to give this information to us.

Do you give your consent for your doctor, or others named in your form, to give DWP, or a healthcare professional approved by the Secretary of State, information relating to your health condition that is relevant to your claim?

{#

We ask for this consent on behalf of your doctor, hospital or relevant professional. This reassures them that they are legally allowed to share this information.

#}
{# I give consent for my doctor, hospital and relevant health care professionals to give information to DWP about my health condition. #}
{# I do not give consent. #}

If you do not give consent

You do not have to give consent. If you choose not to do so, we will make a decision based on the information we have already, as well as any you can give us yourself.

How we use this information

This helps us to decide whether you are able to work or if you can get this benefit.

The law allows us to get, keep and use this information to:

  • process your claim to ESA
  • make a decision on your claim or any mandatory reconsideration or appeal you make

We ask for this consent on behalf of your doctor, hospital or relevant professional. This reassures them that they are legally allowed to share this information.

We can lawfully ask your doctor, hospital, or other relevant professionals for information about your health condition and how it affects you. This is because we are asking for information to help us carry out our official social security functions.

{#

You do not have to give consent. If you choose not to do so, we will make a decision based on:

  • the information we have already
  • any you can give us yourself
#}

If you change your mind

You can change your mind later. Call 0800 169 0310 and say you want to give or withdraw your consent.

If you withdraw your consent, we cannot get information from your doctor or others named on your form.
