Apply for 'new style' Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

You're eligible to apply if you:

  • need money because you're off work with an illness, injury or disability
  • have paid National Insurance in the last 2 tax years
  • do not get Statutory Sick Pay or it's due to end in the next 3 months

If you're not sure whether you've paid enough National Insurance, you can check your National Insurance record online. You'll need to sign in to, or set up, a Government Gateway account.

You'll need a bank or building society account to apply online. You can also use a trusted family member or friend's account.

Find out how to get a bank account if you do not have one.

What to expect

The online form is the first stage of your application.

You'll also need to go to an appointment to prove your identity and agree what you'll do in return for getting Employment and Support Allowance.

You might also need to go to a Work Capability Assessment.

Before you start

Make sure you have your:

  • National Insurance number
  • doctor's surgery name and address
  • bank or building society account details

You'll also need details of:

  • current or recent work
  • the date any Statutory Sick Pay ended or will end
  • pensions that you're getting, including proof of any inherited pensions
  • permanent health insurance or income protection payments
